# Mission: Becoming a strong team leader

> **Status: provisional.** Drafted from a starting assumption because we jumped
> straight into learning. Correct any line and I'll re-aim the whole course.

## Why
You lead (or are about to lead) a small team and want to get strong results
**through** your people — without micromanaging — while keeping their trust.
"Strong leader" here means: the team reliably delivers good work, *and* people
feel safe and motivated while doing it. The two are not a trade-off; the
evidence says trust is what produces the results.

## Success looks like
- You can hand off important work and trust it gets done well — without hovering.
- Your team surfaces problems early instead of hiding them until they're fires.
- You handle hard moments (a tough call, a difficult conversation) with more composure.

## Constraints
- Learning in single, ephemeral sessions for now — no progress is persisted
  between sessions (by your choice). Each session should stand on its own.

## Out of scope (for now)
- Org design, executive strategy, and the legal mechanics of hiring/firing.
  We revisit these once the day-to-day fundamentals are solid.
