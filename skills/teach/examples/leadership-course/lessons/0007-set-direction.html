Strong Team Leadership · Lesson 7 of your course

Set direction people can act on

You can't hand off outcomes (Lesson 1) if no one's clear what the outcomes are. A leader's first product is clarity: a short list of what matters most — and the permission to ignore the rest.

Why this closes the loop

Delegation only works when people know what actually matters. That's the gap this lesson fills. The cleanest tool for it is the OKR — Objectives & Key Results — the system John Doerr carried from Intel into Google in Measure What Matters. [Why OKRs] Done well, it focuses effort, aligns a team, and makes progress measurable instead of a matter of opinion. [Benefits of OKRs]

Direction isn't a speech you give once. It's a short list people can recite — and use to make calls when you're not in the room.

So the closing skill of this course is naming what matters clearly enough that the team can run toward it without you — and clearly enough that they know what to drop.

The tool: write one Objective + 2–3 Key Results

  1. Objective — what you want to achieve, stated qualitatively and memorably. This is the direction. ("Customers trust our product's reliability.")
  2. Key Results — 2–3 measurable outcomes that prove you got there. Numbers, not tasks. ("Crash-free sessions 98% → 99.7%," not "fix the crash backlog.")
  3. Then your real job — repeat it until everyone can recite it, and give context, not control: explain why it matters so people make good calls without you.

Focus is the point. OKRs also tell you what not to do. Doerr's first "superpower" is Focus — the power to say no to everything that doesn't serve the objective. A direction that drops nothing isn't a direction; it's a wish list.

See the difference

✗ Vague direction

"Let's improve quality this quarter."

Nobody knows when they're done, how it's measured, or what to drop to make room for it. Everyone stays busy; nothing moves.

✓ Clear OKR

Objective: Customers trust our product's reliability.

Key Results: Cut P1 bugs from 12 → 3 · Crash-free sessions 98% → 99.7% · Support tickets about errors down 40%.
