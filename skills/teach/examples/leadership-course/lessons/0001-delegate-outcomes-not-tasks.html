Strong Team Leadership · Lesson 1 of your course

Delegate outcomes, not tasks

The fastest way to look like a weak leader is to do, or hover over, work that should belong to someone else. The fix isn't "let go and hope" — it's handing over an outcome so cleanly that hovering becomes unnecessary.

Why this, first

Google studied 180+ teams (Project Aristotle) and found the #1 driver of high performance wasn't who was on the team — it was psychological safety: whether people feel safe to act, speak up, and admit mistakes. [re:Work] Micromanaging is the daily behaviour that quietly destroys it: in one survey, 68% of micromanaged employees reported lower morale and 55% reported lower productivity.

Clarity is kindness. Most "people problems" in a team are actually unclear-expectation problems wearing a costume.

So the foundational skill is transferring ownership of an outcome with enough clarity that the person can run without you — which builds trust and results at the same time.

The tool: the Clear Handoff

Before you delegate anything that matters, say (or better, write) these five things. Miss one and you'll be tempted to hover.

  1. Outcome — the result you want, stated as a finished thing, not a task list. ("A one-page summary the client can approve," not "look into the client thing.")
  2. Why it matters — the context that lets them make good calls you didn't foresee.
  3. Boundaries & decision rights — what's fixed, and what they're free to decide without asking.
  4. Definition of done — how you'll both know it's finished and good.
  5. Check-in cadence — when you'll sync, agreed up front, so you don't "drop by" anxiously.

Set those once and the urge to micromanage mostly evaporates — because the things you'd hover about are already answered.

See the difference

✗ Task dump

"Can you put together some slides about last quarter's numbers for the Thursday meeting? Let me see them before you send anything."

No outcome, no why, no decision rights, no definition of done. You will end up rewriting them.

✓ Clear Handoff

"Goal: leadership leaves Thursday knowing whether we hit Q3 targets and the one risk for Q4 (outcome). It drives the hiring decision (why). Use our deck template; you pick the three metrics that tell the story (decision rights). Done = a 5-slide deck a non-expert understands in 2 minutes (done). Let's review a rough draft Tuesday 3pm (cadence)."
