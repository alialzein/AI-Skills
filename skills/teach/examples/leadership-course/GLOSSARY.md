# Strong Team Leadership — Glossary

The shared language of this course. Each term is the compressed essence of a lesson;
use these words precisely and they'll do a lot of thinking for you.

## Terms

**Clear Handoff** *(Lesson 1)*:
Delegating an *outcome* with the five things that make hovering unnecessary —
Outcome, Why, Boundaries & decision rights, Definition of done, Check-in cadence.
_Avoid_: "passing off work", "assigning a task"

**Outcome (vs. task)** *(Lesson 1)*:
The finished result you want, stated as a thing ("a deck leadership can approve"),
not the activity ("work on the deck"). You delegate outcomes; you assign tasks.

**Psychological safety** *(Lesson 2)*:
The shared belief that you won't be punished for speaking up, admitting a mistake,
or disagreeing. The #1 driver of team performance in Google's Project Aristotle.
_Avoid_: "being nice", "comfort" — safety is about candor, not comfort.

**Frame / Invite / Respond** *(Lesson 2)*:
Edmondson's three moves that build safety: frame the work as learning, invite
participation with real questions, respond productively (thank the messenger).

**Radical Candor** *(Lesson 3)*:
Feedback that does two things at once — Care Personally **and** Challenge Directly.
The opposite failure is Ruinous Empathy.
_Avoid_: "brutal honesty" (that's Obnoxious Aggression — challenge without care).

**Ruinous Empathy** *(Lesson 3)*:
Withholding hard truths to spare someone's short-term feelings. The most common
trap for kind managers, and the one that quietly stalls people's growth.

**The AWE question** *(Lesson 4)*:
"And what else?" — asked 2–3 times before giving advice, because the first answer
is rarely the best one. The core habit for coaching instead of telling.

**Advice monster** *(Lesson 4)*:
The reflex to jump in with your solution. Taming it is what turns a manager into
a coach and keeps the team from becoming dependent on you.

**Two-way door** *(Lesson 5)*:
A reversible, low-cost decision — decide it fast. Contrast with a **one-way door**
(hard to undo), which deserves slow, careful analysis. (Bezos.)

**Disagree and commit** *(Lesson 5)*:
Argue your case hard before the decision; once the decider calls it, commit fully —
even if you disagreed. Prevents quiet relitigating.

**Facts vs. story** *(Lesson 6)*:
The discipline of separating what observably happened (facts) from the
interpretation you've layered on top (story). Lead hard conversations with facts;
hold the story tentatively.

**Make it safe** *(Lesson 6)*:
In a tense conversation, restoring the other person's sense of respect and shared
purpose so they stop going silent or attacking. Safety first, content second.

**Objective & Key Results (OKR)** *(Lesson 7)*:
An **Objective** (memorable, qualitative direction) plus 2–3 **Key Results**
(measurable outcomes that prove you got there). The leader's tool for clarity.

**Context, not control** *(Lesson 7)*:
Giving people the *why* and the priorities so they can make good calls on their own —
the alternative to micromanaging. Direction-setting is what makes delegation safe.
